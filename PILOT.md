# EGW Chapter Context — Pilot Proposal

*A one-page ask for a Sabbath School teacher, academy Bible teacher, or youth leader.*

## What it is

A free, offline-capable web app that adds a **study layer** around Ellen White's classic
writings — the layer the official EGW Writings app doesn't provide:

- **Historical context** — why each chapter was written (e.g. Steps to Christ against the
  backdrop of 1888 Minneapolis and Victorian fear-of-God religion)
- **Argument flow** — the chapter's reasoning in plain steps
- **Hard phrases** — Victorian vocabulary explained for modern readers
- **Common misunderstanding** — the misreading each chapter most often suffers
- **Bible foundation** — the KJV texts the chapter stands on
- **Discussion questions and quizzes** — ready to use in a class

Every quotation from historians and scholars carries a verification badge (Verbatim /
Paraphrase / Summary / Not audited) with a link to the original source. Ellen White's own
words always link back to egwwritings.org.

## What it is not

Not affiliated with the White Estate. Not a replacement for the books. Not theologically
reviewed yet — that's part of what this pilot is for.

## The ask (4 weeks, ~zero prep)

1. Pick a class already studying **Steps to Christ** (all 13 chapters are covered).
2. Before each week's chapter, spend 5 minutes on that chapter's context page; use one or
   two of its discussion questions in class.
3. Optionally let students take the chapter quiz on their own devices.
4. At the end, answer three questions:
   - Did the context section change how the chapter landed with the class?
   - Was anything theologically off or overstated? (Please be blunt.)
   - Would you keep using it, and for which book next?

## What the feedback decides

- Whether to complete The Great Controversy (currently 4 of 42 chapters)
- Which claims need a reviewing pastor/teacher's sign-off before wider sharing
- Whether quizzes and discussion questions match real classroom use
